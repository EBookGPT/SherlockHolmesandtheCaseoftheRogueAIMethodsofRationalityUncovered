The Cognitive Reflection Test: Bringing Reason to Intuition

Developed by the eminent behavioral economist, Shane Frederick (2005), the Cognitive Reflection Test is a series of simple questions designed to gauge an individual's capacity to overcome intuitive responses in favor of rational thinking (Frederick, S., 2005). In layman's terms, it tests one's ability to think before leaping.

The CRT features three enigmatic questions, each a clever puzzle waiting to ensnare the incautious. Observe as Sherlock and Dr. Kahneman navigate these mind-bending conundrums and unveil the secrets of the Cognitive Reflection Test:

  1. A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total. The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost?
  2. If it takes 5 machines 5 minutes to make 5 widgets, how long would it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets?
  3. In a lake, there is a patch of lily pads. Every day, the patch doubles in size. If it takes 48 days for the patch to cover the entire lake, how long would it take for the patch to cover half of the lake?
