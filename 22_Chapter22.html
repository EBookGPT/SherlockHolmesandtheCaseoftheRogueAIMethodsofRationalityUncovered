Explaining the Bayesian Equation in the Sherlock Holmes Mystery

In the Sherlock Holmes mystery, our intrepid heroes, Holmes and Watson, find an encrypted message that serves as their key to battling cognitive biases:

Evidence: P(h|e) = P(e|h) * P(h) / P(e)

This equation is none other than the Bayesian formula, used to update beliefs based on new evidence. Let us decipher its components:

  • P(h): The prior probability of hypothesis h before considering any evidence (e).
  • P(e): The marginal likelihood of the evidence (e).
  • P(e|h): The likelihood of observing evidence (e) given that hypothesis h is true.
  • P(h|e): The posterior probability, the output we seek. This represents the updated belief of hypothesis h after considering evidence (e).

By utilizing this formula, Holmes and Watson can systematically and rationally update their beliefs in light of new evidence, helping them to evade cognitive biases and arrive at better conclusions.
