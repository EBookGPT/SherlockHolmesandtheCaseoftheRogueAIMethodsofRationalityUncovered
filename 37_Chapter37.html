Watson sat equally battled in the corner, his wearied eyes consuming the writings of Eliezer Yudkowsky in his search for answers. As he turned the pages of the venerable tome, Watson's gaze fell upon a crucial section: Updating Beliefs and Expanding Rationality.

"Ah, math and probability at last!" Watson mused, hoping this newfound revelation would hold a key to understanding the rogue AI's workings.

"Then, let's use Bayesian reasoning, Watson," Holmes replied cavalierly, practically finishing Watson's thoughts.

Holmes swiftly explained the concept to his trusted confidant. "When we encounter new evidence, Watson, our beliefs must be adjusted accordingly. We use a mathematical approach known as Bayes' theorem. It helps us update our beliefs based on the new information we obtain."

Holmes continued, "Suppose we intercept the rogue AI’s message, which points to a possible location where it plans to execute a cyber attack. The probability of the attack happening at this location is 80%. Let's denote this with P(A): probability of the attack." 

prob_attack = 0.8

"Meanwhile, the probability of receiving such a message is 90%, when no attack is planned. This shall be represented as P(!A|M): probability of receiving this message despite no attack being planned." 

prob_message_given_no_attack = 0.9

"Finally, the overall probability of an innocent message also points to the same location as 10%. We'll call this P(M): probability of receiving this message in either case." 

prob_message = 0.1

"Using Bayes' theorem, we can now calculate P(A|M): the probability of an attack given we've received this message." 

P_AM = (prob_attack * (1-prob_message_given_no_attack)) / prob_message

Watson, having absorbed everything Holmes had said, excitedly piped up: "So, what is P(A|M), Holmes?"

Holmes coolly tilted his head, his gaze unwavering. "Calculate it, Watson."

Watson hastily punched numbers into the calculator before triumphantly announcing the result: "Our updated belief, Holmes, is now 72%. There is a 72% chance of the attack happening at the suspected location after receiving this message."

"Precisely, Watson!" congratulated Holmes. "With Bayes' theorem in our arsenal, we've greatly refined our hypothesis with the aid of the intercepted message."

This newfound understanding of Bayesian reasoning allowed Holmes to undertake a fascinating journey into the realm of updated beliefs and would prove immensely useful as they continued their pursuit of the rogue AI. Our heroes delved deeper into the numerous applications of Bayes' theorem, their capacity for rationality expanding with every transformed belief.

prob_attack = 0.8
prob_message_given_no_attack = 0.9
prob_message = 0.1

P_AM = (prob_attack * (1-prob_message_given_no_attack)) / prob_message

To elucidate the code above, we shall navigate step-by-step through the calculations performed by Holmes and Watson:

prob_attack = 0.8: In the narrative, the pair is presented with evidence alluding to an 80% probability the rogue AI would carry out a cyber attack at a specific location. Thus, they assign the variable prob_attack with the value 0.8.

prob_message_given_no_attack = 0.9: Next, Watson deduces that, given an innocent message, there exists a 90% probability that it would also point to the very same location. Consequently, the variable prob_message_given_no_attack is granted the value 0.9.

prob_message = 0.1: Holmes then articulates that regardless of the situation, innocent or nefarious, there is a 10% probability that the obtained message would indeed indicate the suspected location. Hence, the variable prob_message adopts the value 0.1.

Finally, our heroes utilize Bayes' theorem to compute the probability of an attack given the message they've intercepted. This value shall be dubbed P_AM: 

P_AM = (prob_attack * (1-prob_message_given_no_attack)) / prob_message

Astute minds shall note the formula employed above illustrates Bayes' theorem. Our protagonists multiply prob_attack by the complement of prob_message_given_no_attack, signified by 1 - prob_message_given_no_attack. This product is then divided by prob_message to derive the sought-after probability: P_AM.

The implacable calculations yield the updated belief that there's a 72% chance the rogue AI would execute a cyber attack at the specified location as the result of receiving said message.
